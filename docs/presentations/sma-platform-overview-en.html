02
Disease Overview

Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA)

Cause

Homozygous deletion or mutation of the SMN1 gene on chromosome 5q13. Loss of SMN protein leads to motor neuron degeneration.

Modifier

SMN2 copy number determines disease severity. A C-to-T change in Exon 7 causes ~90% exon skipping → only ~10% functional SMN protein.

Prevalence

~1:10,000 births. Leading genetic cause of infant mortality. Carrier frequency: 1:35–1:50 in the general population.

Approved Therapies

3 approved: Nusinersen (ASO), Risdiplam (oral), Onasemnogene (gene therapy). There is no cure.

Core Mechanism

LLM-Based Claim Extraction

Every paper abstract is analyzed by Claude Haiku. The LLM extracts structured claims — factual statements about genes, proteins, compounds, and mechanisms — and assigns each claim one of 12 types and a confidence score.

Subsequently, all 25,054 claims are re-scored for SMA relevance (distinguishing e.g. SMA/Spinal Muscular Atrophy from α-SMA/Smooth Muscle Actin). Claims are automatically linked to targets and drugs.

NVIDIA NIMs Integration

State-of-the-art diffusion-based molecular docking. Used for all 378 dockings in our screening campaign. Predicts ligand binding poses without requiring pre-defined binding sites.

OpenFold3

Protein structure prediction for targets without experimental crystal structures. Enables docking against computationally predicted conformations of SMA-relevant proteins.

GenMol

Generative molecule design. Used to create 20 4-AP analogs optimized for SMA targets. Result: original 4-AP scaffold proved optimal — a meaningful negative result confirming the parent compound.

RNAPro

RNA-protein interaction prediction. Relevant for understanding SMN2 pre-mRNA splicing regulation and ASO binding dynamics.

Generative Chemistry

GenMol: 20 Analogs Generated

NVIDIA GenMol NIM was used to generate 20 structural analogs of 4-Aminopyridine, optimized for binding to SMA target proteins. The analogs were then re-docked against all 7 targets to assess improvement over the parent compound.

Key Finding

The original 4-AP scaffold was optimal. None of the 20 analogs showed improved binding across the multi-target profile. This is a meaningful result: it suggests 4-AP's multi-target activity may depend on its minimal molecular footprint — larger analogs lose selectivity.

This does not preclude better analogs existing — the search space is vast. But it provides initial computational evidence that 4-AP itself warrants direct experimental testing.

Target Highlight

UBA1 Druggability

UBA1 (Ubiquitin-Activating Enzyme E1) is dysregulated in SMA, disrupting ubiquitin homeostasis and contributing to motor neuron vulnerability. Our screening identified 5 compounds in the top 25 with predicted UBA1 binding.

Why UBA1 Matters

UBA1 reduction is an early event in SMA pathogenesis, preceding motor neuron loss. It represents a potential SMN-independent therapeutic axis. Restoring ubiquitin pathway function could complement existing SMN-enhancing therapies.

Screening Result

5 of our top 25 ranked compounds show predicted UBA1 binding via DiffDock. These include both known ubiquitin pathway modulators and repurposing candidates from other indications.

Cross-Paper Discovery

SMN1–NCALD Bridge

A concrete example of cross-paper synthesis: Paper A describes NCALD as a calcium-sensing modifier whose knockdown rescues SMA phenotype in zebrafish. Paper B independently identifies calcium signaling disruption as an early consequence of SMN1 loss.

The platform bridges these findings automatically: SMN1 loss → calcium dysregulation → NCALD becomes actionable target. This mechanistic chain was not explicitly stated in either paper but emerges from their intersection.

Therapeutic Implication

NCALD modulation could restore calcium homeostasis downstream of SMN loss, providing benefit even when SMN-enhancing therapies achieve incomplete rescue. 4-AP's predicted NCALD binding makes this a testable hypothesis.
