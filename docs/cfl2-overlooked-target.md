## 2. What CFL2 Does

Cofilin-2 (CFL2) is the muscle-predominant isoform of the cofilin family of actin-depolymerizing factors. It:

- **Severs and depolymerizes F-actin filaments**, controlling actin turnover
- Is **essential for sarcomere maintenance** in skeletal muscle
- Is regulated by **phosphorylation at Ser3** by LIMK1/2 (inactivation) and dephosphorylation by SSH1/chronophin (activation)
- When dysregulated, forms **actin-cofilin rods** that sequester actin and cofilin, depleting the cellular actin pool

The related isoform CFL1 (cofilin-1, non-muscle/neuronal) is better studied in neurodegeneration. CFL2's role at the neuromuscular junction and in motor neuron-muscle crosstalk is largely unexplored.

## 3. The Smoking Gun: Actin-Cofilin Rods Already Found in SMA

**PMID:33986363** (Walter et al., 2021, Scientific Reports) -- "Profilin2 regulates actin rod assembly in neuronal cells"

This paper directly demonstrates:
- **Actin-cofilin rods form in SMA cell culture models**
- Rods sequester proteins involved in ubiquitination, translation, and protein folding
- **Profilin2 (PFN2) and its upstream effectors RhoA/ROCK regulate rod assembly in SMA**
- Rod persistence impairs motoneuron homeostasis by disrupting actin dynamics and essential cellular pathways

This is the critical link: SMN deficiency --> dysregulated RhoA/ROCK --> aberrant cofilin phosphorylation --> actin-cofilin rod formation --> motor neuron damage.

## 4. The ROCK-LIMK-Cofilin Axis in SMA

### SMN is directly linked to ROCK pathway via profilin

**PMID:21920940** (Nolle et al., 2011, Human Molecular Genetics) -- "The spinal muscular atrophy disease protein SMN is linked to the Rho-kinase pathway via profilin"

Key findings:
- SMN **directly interacts with profilin2a** via a proline-rich stretch
- An SMA patient mutation (S230L) specifically disrupts this interaction
- In SMA models: **differential phosphorylation of ROCK-downstream targets cofilin, myosin-light chain phosphatase, and profilin2a**
- **Hyper-phosphorylation of profilin2a** links SMN to the ROCK pathway
- **Neuron-specific increase in F-/G-actin ratio** in SMA

### Cytoskeleton dysfunction review (2024)

**PMID:39666039** (Shi et al., 2024, J Neurol) -- "Cytoskeleton dysfunction of motor neuron in spinal muscular atrophy"

This comprehensive review confirms:
- SMN ablation disrupts actin dynamics, growth cone formation, axonal stability, neurite outgrowth
- Key modulators: PFN2, PLS3, STMN1, MAP1B, neurofilament
- **CFL2 is NOT mentioned** -- further confirming it is overlooked
- The review explicitly suggests cytoskeleton dynamics as "a promising approach to develop new therapy or biomarker"

## 5. Why CFL2 Matters More Than CFL1 in SMA

While CFL1 (cofilin-1) is the neuronal isoform and gets most attention in neurodegeneration research, CFL2 may be more relevant to SMA because:

1. **SMA is a neuromuscular disease, not just a neuronal one**: SMN deficiency causes intrinsic muscle defects independent of denervation (PMID:39596480)
2. **CFL2 is the muscle isoform**: It controls sarcomere maintenance and muscle actin dynamics
3. **CFL2 2.9x upregulation suggests compensatory response**: The muscle may be trying to increase actin turnover to compensate for SMN-related cytoskeletal defects
4. **CFL2 mutations cause nemaline myopathy** (PMID:40581737): Biallelic CFL2 variants cause an ultra-rare myopathy with nemaline rods -- pathologically similar to actin-cofilin rods seen in SMA
5. **Muscle cofilin controls NMJ development** (PMID:38869008): Drosophila cofilin knockdown in muscle causes F-actin disorganization in the postsynaptic NMJ compartment, mislocalization of glutamate receptors, and impaired NMJ transmission

## 6. The ALS Parallel: Cofilin Hyperphosphorylation Triggers TDP-43

**PMID:41804798** (Jagaraj et al., 2026, Brain) -- "Cofilin hyperphosphorylation triggers TDP-43 pathology in sporadic amyotrophic lateral sclerosis"

This brand-new paper (March 2026) shows:
- **Cofilin hyperphosphorylation directly triggers TDP-43 pathology** in ALS
- This is mediated through actin cytoskeleton disruption
- Provides mechanistic link between LIMK/cofilin axis and protein aggregation pathology

**Relevance to SMA**: While TDP-43 pathology is not a hallmark of SMA, this paper proves that cofilin dysregulation has consequences far beyond actin dynamics -- it triggers protein homeostasis collapse. In SMA, the equivalent downstream effect may be actin-cofilin rod formation and sequestration of essential proteins.

## 7. Is the LIMK-->CFL2 Axis Druggable?

### Yes -- multiple lines of evidence

#### A. ROCK inhibitors (upstream of LIMK)

**Fasudil** -- already in ALS clinical trials:
- **PMID:40950480** (Wolff et al., 2025, medRxiv): Post-hoc analysis of the **ROCK-ALS trial** shows fasudil significantly reduced the number of newly affected muscles at day 90 in a dose-dependent manner
- 17 papers exist for "Rho kinase SMA spinal muscular atrophy" -- the pathway is recognized but not targeted clinically in SMA
- 12 papers for "fasudil motor neuron neuroprotection"

**Fasudil in SMA rationale**: Since SMN deficiency causes ROCK pathway hyperactivation (PMID:21920940), and fasudil is already shown to attenuate motor neuron loss in ALS, testing fasudil in SMA models should be a priority.

#### B. LIMK inhibitors (direct CFL2 regulators)

**PMID:40765414** (Baldwin et al., 2025, J Med Chem): A novel class of **highly selective LIMK inhibitors** (tetrahydropyrazolopyridinones) has been developed:
- **MDI-117740**: Potent dual LIMK1/2 inhibitor
- Significantly improved DMPK properties suitable for in vivo evaluation
- Most selective LIMK inhibitor reported to date (minimal kinome off-targets)
- Designed for neurodegenerative disease applications

53 papers exist for "LIMK inhibitor cancer actin" -- the field is active with multiple chemical series. Two reviews cover the clinical landscape:
- PMID:31030376: "Targeting ROCK/LIMK/cofilin signaling pathway in cancer"
- PMID:29908439: "Recent advances in rational design of LIM kinase inhibitors"

#### C. Cofilin-targeting peptides

**PMID:41298223** (Lin et al., 2026, Neurotherapeutics): A **16-amino acid peptide** derived from phosphoglycerate kinase 1 (Pgk1):
- Reduces phospho-Cofilin levels in motor neurons
- **Delays motor neuron degeneration in ALS models**
- Administration of extracellular Pgk1 (ePgk1) reduces motor neuron pathology

## 8. Do Cofilin-Rods Form in SMA Like in ALS?

**Critical difference**: In SMA, rod formation has a **direct genetic link** to disease (SMN-->profilin2a-->ROCK-->cofilin), making it potentially a more specific and tractable target than in ALS where the trigger is more heterogeneous.

## 9. Proposed Hypothesis

### CFL2 Upregulation as Maladaptive Compensation in SMA

**Hypothesis**: The 2.9x upregulation of CFL2 in SMA motor neurons represents an initially compensatory but ultimately maladaptive response to SMN-mediated actin cytoskeleton disruption. Excess CFL2, when inappropriately phosphorylated via the hyperactive ROCK/LIMK pathway, becomes a substrate for actin-cofilin rod formation, creating a vicious cycle that:

1. Depletes the functional actin pool
2. Sequesters essential proteins (ubiquitin pathway, translation machinery)
3. Disrupts NMJ postsynaptic actin organization
4. Impairs synaptic transmission at the neuromuscular junction

### Testable predictions:
- CFL2 knockdown in SMA motor neurons should **reduce actin rod formation**
- CFL2 phosphorylation status (p-CFL2/total CFL2 ratio) should be **elevated in SMA tissue**
- LIMK inhibition should **normalize CFL2 activity** and reduce rod formation
- Fasudil should **ameliorate NMJ defects** in SMA mouse models
- CFL2 levels could serve as a **peripheral biomarker** (measurable in muscle biopsy or potentially plasma)

## 12. Bottom Line

CFL2 sits at the intersection of three established SMA pathomechanisms:
1. **Actin cytoskeleton disruption** (SMN-->profilin2a-->ROCK-->cofilin)
2. **NMJ dysfunction** (cofilin controls postsynaptic actin organization)
3. **Intrinsic muscle pathology** (CFL2 is the muscle isoform; its dysregulation causes myopathy)

The LIMK-->CFL2 axis is druggable TODAY:
- **Fasudil** (ROCK inhibitor): Already in ALS phase 2, shows motor neuron protection
- **MDI-117740** (LIMK inhibitor): Novel, highly selective, in vivo ready (2025)
- **Pgk1-derived peptide**: Reduces p-cofilin, delays motor neuron degeneration

**No one has connected these dots for SMA.** This represents a genuine first-in-field opportunity.
