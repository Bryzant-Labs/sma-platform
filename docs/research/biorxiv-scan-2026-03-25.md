## Category A: Directly Relevant to ROCK-LIMK-Cofilin Axis

### 1. Fasudil Attenuates Disease Spreading in ALS — Post-hoc Analysis of ROCK-ALS Trial

- **DOI**: 10.1101/2025.09.02.25334770
- **URL**: https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.09.02.25334770v1
- **Authors**: ROCK-ALS investigators (Munich/Germany, France, Switzerland)
- **Date**: September 2025 (medRxiv)
- **Journal**: Published in Lancet Neurology (primary: PMID 39424560); post-hoc on medRxiv
- **Key findings**:
  - Phase 2 RCT: 120 patients (fasudil 30mg, 60mg, or placebo as add-on to riluzole)
  - **Fasudil significantly reduced newly affected muscles** at day 90, dose-dependently
  - MUNIX (Motor Unit Number Index) decline attenuated at day 45 and 90
  - No serious adverse events — excellent safety/tolerability
  - Sex-specific effect on lung function decline (significant in women at 60mg)
  - ALSFRS-R and NfL not significantly different (short treatment duration noted)
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL — validates ROCK inhibition as disease-modifying in motor neuron disease. Directly supports our fasudil-for-SMA hypothesis (Bowerman 2012 extension).
- **Status in DB**: NOT FOUND — needs addition

### 2. Dysregulated Actin Dynamics and Cofilin Correlate with TDP-43 Pathology in Sporadic ALS

- **DOI**: 10.1101/2023.08.28.555209
- **URL**: https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2023.08.28.555209v1.full
- **Date**: August 2023 (bioRxiv)
- **Key findings**:
  - **Increased phospho-LIMK1/2 and phospho-cofilin** in sporadic ALS patient spinal cords
  - More F-actin (polymerized) relative to G-actin in lumbar spinal cords from ALS patients
  - TDP-43 physically interacts with actin
  - Pharmacological induction of actin polymerization produces TDP-43 pathology features (cytoplasmic mis-localization, inclusions, stress granules)
  - Increased Tpm 4.2 and Rac1/cdc42 in ALS tissue
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL — directly implicates LIMK/cofilin axis in ALS motor neuron pathology. Validates our ROCK-LIMK2-CFL2 therapeutic axis concept across diseases (SMA + ALS convergence).
- **Status in DB**: NOT FOUND — needs addition

### 3. SAFE-ROCK: Phase I Trial of Oral Fasudil

- **DOI**: 10.1007/s40263-024-01070-7
- **PMID**: 38416402
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40263-024-01070-7
- **Date**: February 2024 (CNS Drugs)
- **Key findings**:
  - 14 healthy volunteers, single-center, open-label crossover
  - Oral fasudil **69% bioavailability** (active metabolite hydroxyfasudil)
  - No serious adverse events at 90 mg/day
  - Fasudil concentrations highly variable (CV 41%)
  - Dose adjustments needed for chronic application
- **Relevance**: HIGH — establishes oral fasudil feasibility for chronic neurodegeneration. Groundwork for ROCK-PD (Parkinson's) and potential SMA trials.
- **Status in DB**: NOT FOUND — needs addition

### 4. Fasudil Inhibits Alpha-Synuclein Aggregation Through ROCK-Inhibition-Mediated Mechanisms

- **PMID**: 39915220
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12014416/
- **Date**: February 2025
- **Key findings**:
  - Fasudil decreases alpha-synuclein inclusions in dopaminergic neurons and glia
  - Effect is ROCK-mediated (confirmed with Y27632 and ROCK gene silencing)
  - ROCK inhibition downregulates NADPH-oxidase-derived oxidative stress, intracellular calcium, and alpha-synuclein endocytosis
  - Promotes autophagy
- **Relevance**: HIGH — expands ROCK inhibition therapeutic scope beyond motor neuron diseases. Validates multi-disease utility of our target pathway.

### 5. MDI-114215: A Potent and Selective LIMK Inhibitor for Fragile X Syndrome

- **DOI**: 10.1021/acs.jmedchem.4c02694
- **PMID**: 39711116
- **URL**: https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.jmedchem.4c02694
- **Authors**: Baldwin et al. (Oxford CMD / Dundee)
- **Date**: January 2025 (J Med Chem)
- **Key findings**:
  - **Allosteric dual LIMK1/2 inhibitor** with exquisite kinome selectivity
  - Reduces phospho-cofilin in mouse brain slices
  - **Rescues impaired hippocampal LTP** in Fragile X Syndrome mice
  - Effective in iPSC neurons from FXS patients
  - CNS-penetrant small molecule
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL — first drug-quality LIMK inhibitor with CNS proof-of-concept. Validates LIMK as druggable target for neurological disorders. Could be tested in SMA models (LIMK2 pathway).

### 10. Combining SMN2 Splicing Modifiers with HDAC6 Inhibition Greatly Improves SMA

- **DOI**: 10.1101/2025.01.15.633267
- **URL**: https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.01.15.633267v1
