## 1. MAPK14 Champion: 4-Cyanophenyl Norbornane Thiadiazole

**SMILES:** `N#Cc1ccc(-c2nnc(NC(=O)[C@H]3[C@@H]4CCCC[C@@H]43)s2)cc1`
**Scaffold:** 1,3,4-thiadiazole + norbornane (bicyclo[2.2.1]heptane) + 4-cyanophenyl
**Target:** p38 MAPK (MAPK14)

### 1.1 Thiadiazole Scaffold Patents

| Aspect | Finding | Risk |
|--------|---------|------|
| 1,3,4-Thiadiazole as kinase inhibitor scaffold | Well-established in patent literature (2005-2016 review covers broad applications). Used for c-Met kinase, CK2, FAK, HDAC, Src/Abl tyrosine kinases. | LOW — scaffold is generic/privileged, widely used across many targets |
| Thiadiazole + p38 MAPK | Japanese patent JP2015155389A covers p38 MAP kinase gamma/delta inhibitors with thiadiazole. No specific p38-alpha thiadiazole patent identified. | LOW-MEDIUM — gamma/delta selectivity differs from our alpha target |
| Thiadiazole + LIMK inhibitor | WO2014002101A1 (2014) covers LIM kinase inhibitors binding ATP/substrate sites. Specific structures unclear from abstract. | NEEDS REVIEW — check if thiadiazole is claimed as LIMK scaffold |

### 1.2 Norbornane/Bicyclo[2.2.1]heptane Scaffold Patents

| Aspect | Finding | Risk |
|--------|---------|------|
| Norbornane in kinase inhibitors | Used as a privileged bridged bicyclic scaffold. WO2014173289A1 claims bicyclo[2.2.1]heptane in fused heterocyclic protein kinase inhibitors. WO2015150995A1 claims norbornane in IRAK4 inhibitors. | LOW-MEDIUM — used broadly but specific kinase+norbornane combinations may be claimed |
| Norbornane + p38 MAPK | No specific patent found claiming norbornane scaffolds for p38 MAPK inhibition | LOW |

### 1.3 Combined Scaffold (4-Cyanophenyl + Thiadiazole + Norbornane)

| Aspect | Finding | Risk |
|--------|---------|------|
| Exact combination | No patent found claiming this specific three-component scaffold | LOW — novel combination |
| 4-Cyanophenyl + thiadiazole | German patent DE4218157A1 describes thiadiazolines with 4-cyanophenyl substituents (not kinase context) | LOW — different chemical class and target |
| Norbornane + thiadiazole amide | No specific patent identified | LOW — appears novel |

### 1.4 FTO Assessment for MAPK14 Champion

**Overall Risk: LOW**

The compound combines three well-known medicinal chemistry building blocks (thiadiazole, norbornane, cyanophenyl) in a novel arrangement. No existing patent was found that specifically claims this structural combination for p38 MAPK inhibition. The p38 MAPK inhibitor patent landscape is mature (20+ clinical candidates, none approved except pirfenidone which is weak/unselective), meaning many early composition-of-matter patents are expiring. The specific combination of a norbornane carboxamide linked to a 1,3,4-thiadiazole bearing a 4-cyanophenyl group appears to be novel and potentially patentable.

**Key risk:** Broad Markush claims in existing p38 MAPK patents could theoretically encompass this scaffold. Full claim review of the 2014-2019 p38 MAPK patent review (Pubmed: 32228113) is recommended.

## 2. Multi-Kinase Leads: MKI-013, MKI-008, MKI-003

**Targets:** ROCK2 + MAPK14 dual inhibition

### 2.1 ROCK Inhibitor Patent Landscape

| Aspect | Finding | Risk |
|--------|---------|------|

| US9815820B2 | Rho kinase inhibitors patent (Google Patents) | NEEDS REVIEW — check structural claims |

### 2.2 Dual ROCK2 + MAPK14 Inhibitor Patents

| Aspect | Finding | Risk |
|--------|---------|------|
| Dual ROCK2/MAPK14 inhibitor | No specific patent found claiming this dual-target combination | LOW — novel therapeutic concept |
| Multi-kinase inhibitor combinations for SMA | No patent found | LOW — appears novel |
| Multi-kinase inhibitor combinations (general) | Broad concept patented for oncology (e.g., sorafenib, sunitinib classes) but not for SMA/neurodegeneration | LOW for SMA indication |

### 2.3 FTO Assessment for Multi-Kinase Leads

**Overall Risk: LOW-MEDIUM**

The dual ROCK2+MAPK14 inhibition concept for SMA appears novel with no existing patent coverage. However, the individual ROCK inhibitor patent landscape is active (20+ patents 2017-2023), so the specific chemical structures of MKI-013, MKI-008, and MKI-003 need to be checked against existing ROCK inhibitor Markush claims. The SMA indication adds novelty.

**Action required:** Compare the specific chemical structures of each MKI compound against the 2017-2023 ROCK inhibitor patent claims.

## 3. Fasudil Repurposing

### 3.1 Original Fasudil Patent Status

| Aspect | Finding | Risk |
|--------|---------|------|
| Original molecule | Developed by Asahi Kasei in 1984, approved Japan 1995 | EXPIRED |
| Composition of matter patent | Expired ~2016 | CLEAR |
| Formulation patent | Expired ~2019 | CLEAR |
| Generic IV fasudil | Available in Japan and China for cerebral vasospasm | CLEAR |
| Fasudil hydrochloride synthesis | US patent application 20240287027 (Aug 2024) filed for preparation method | LOW — synthesis method patents don't block use |

### 3.2 Woolsey Pharmaceuticals / BRAVYL (Oral Fasudil) Patents

**THIS IS THE MAIN FTO CONCERN FOR FASUDIL REPURPOSING**

| Patent Number | Coverage | Status | Risk to SMA Use |
|---------------|----------|--------|-----------------|
| US 11,779,588 | Method of treating sporadic ALS with oral fasudil/hydroxyfasudil | Granted | MEDIUM — ALS-specific, not SMA |
| US 12,128,053 | Treating ALS using dysphagia-friendly fasudil formulation | Granted | LOW — ALS-specific formulation |
| US 11,944,633 | Dysphagia-friendly solid/liquid fasudil formulations | Granted | MEDIUM — formulation claims may be broad |
| US 12,115,167 | Dysphagia-friendly fasudil formulations | Granted | MEDIUM — formulation claims may be broad |

**Additional context:**
- Woolsey has a **36-patent-family portfolio** for fasudil (many pending)
- Woolsey licensed fasudil from Asahi Kasei in 2019 for non-ophthalmic, non-IV formulations outside Japan/China/South Korea/Taiwan
- Their patents focus on ALS indication + oral formulation
- Their claims include NfL reduction as a biomarker endpoint
- Woolsey completed B Round funding (Feb 2025) and has Phase 2a REAL trial data (180mg and 300mg doses)

### 3.3 SMA-Specific Fasudil Use

| Aspect | Finding | Risk |
|--------|---------|------|
| Fasudil for SMA (academic) | Published: Bowerman et al. 2012 (BMC Medicine) showed fasudil improves survival in SMA mice | CLEAR for research |
| Patent on fasudil for SMA | No specific patent found claiming fasudil for SMA treatment | LOW |
| Woolsey SMA coverage | Woolsey patents appear ALS-specific, not covering SMA or other neurodegenerative diseases | LOW-MEDIUM |

### 3.4 FTO Assessment for Fasudil Repurposing

**Overall Risk: MEDIUM**

- **IV fasudil for SMA:** LOW risk — original patents expired, generic available
- **Oral fasudil for SMA:** MEDIUM risk — Woolsey's oral formulation patents could create obstacles even though their method-of-treatment claims are ALS-specific. Their 36-patent-family portfolio likely contains broader claims that need review.
- **Novel fasudil formulation for SMA:** If developing own formulation (not dysphagia-friendly oral), risk is LOW
- **Academic/research use:** CLEAR — no patent blocks research

**Critical question:** Do any of Woolsey's 36 patent families contain claims broad enough to cover oral fasudil for neurodegenerative diseases generally (not just ALS)? This requires full claim review.

## 4. MW150

### 4.1 Patent Status

| Aspect | Finding | Risk |
|--------|---------|------|
| Core patent | US 9,783,525 B2 — "MAP kinase modulators and uses thereof" | ACTIVE — HIGH risk |
| Assignees | Northwestern University + Columbia University | Academic IP, licensable |
| Inventors | D. Martin Watterson, Saktimayee M. Roy, Jeffrey C. Pelletier, Ottavio Arancio | Key researchers |
| Patent family | WO2014145485A2 (international) + US20160130253A1 (pub) + US9783525B2 (granted) | Broad family |
| Chemical structure | Pyridazine scaffold: 6-(4-methylpiperazin-1-yl)-3-(naphthalen-2-yl)-4-(pyridin-4-yl)pyridazine | Specific compound claimed |
| Claims scope | Pyridazine derivatives that selectively inhibit p38alpha MAPK. Structural requirements include naphthyl and pyridinyl groups. | Covers MW150 compound class |
| Development | Licensed to NeuroKine Therapeutics LLC | Commercial rights taken |
| Clinical status | Phase 2 in mild-to-moderate Alzheimer's disease (NCT05194163) | Active development |

### 4.2 MW150 for SMA Indication

| Aspect | Finding | Risk |
|--------|---------|------|
| SMA use published | Carlini, Pellizzoni et al. (2025) EMBO Mol Med — MW150 as neuroprotective in SMA mice | Published validation |
| SMA indication in patent? | Original patent (US9783525) claims cover neurological/neuroinflammatory disorders broadly — SMA may be within scope | HIGH |
| Combination therapy patent? | No specific patent found for MW150 + SMN-inducing drug combination | MEDIUM — likely filed or pending given 2025 publication |

### 4.3 FTO Assessment for MW150

**Overall Risk: HIGH — Cannot freely use MW150**

MW150 is patented composition of matter by Northwestern/Columbia and licensed to NeuroKine Therapeutics. The compound cannot be commercially developed for SMA without a license. However:

- **Academic research:** Likely permissible under research exemption
- **SMA as new indication:** The 2025 EMBO paper (Pellizzoni lab at Columbia, which is a co-assignee) validates MW150 for SMA. Columbia likely has interest in SMA development.
- **Licensing opportunity:** Since Columbia is both a patent co-holder and the source of SMA validation data, a licensing discussion for SMA indication is the correct path
- **Our own p38 MAPK inhibitor (thiadiazole compound):** This is structurally distinct from the pyridazine MW150 scaffold and would NOT infringe the MW150 patent

**Strategic implication:** Our novel MAPK14 champion (4-cyanophenyl norbornane thiadiazole) is a **freedom-to-operate alternative** to MW150 for p38 MAPK inhibition in SMA. This is a significant competitive advantage.
