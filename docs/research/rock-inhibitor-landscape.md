# ROCK/LIMK Inhibitor Landscape for SMA

**Date**: 2026-03-24
**Purpose**: Comprehensive review of ROCK and LIMK inhibitors with relevance to SMA therapeutics
**For**: Meeting with Prof. Schoeneberg's group

## Executive Summary

- **13 compounds** identified targeting the ROCK/LIMK pathway (10 ROCK inhibitors, 3 LIMK inhibitors)
- **4 with demonstrated CNS penetration** (Fasudil, Y-27632, Belumosudil, H-1152)
- **3 FDA/EMA-approved** for other indications (Belumosudil, Netarsudil, Ripasudil)
- **1 approved in Japan/China** with active neurological trials (Fasudil)
- **1 compound directly validated in SMA** (Fasudil; Bowerman 2012, PMID 22397316)
- **Critical gap**: No LIMK2-selective inhibitor exists despite LIMK2 (not LIMK1) being the relevant kinase in SMA motor neurons

## Fasudil -- Lead Candidate for SMA

### Preclinical Evidence in SMA

#### Bowerman et al. 2012 (PMID 22397316)
**"Fasudil improves survival and promotes skeletal muscle development in a mouse model of spinal muscular atrophy"**
- **Journal**: BMC Medicine
- **Model**: Smn2B/- mouse (intermediate SMA model)
- **Dose**: 30 mg/kg twice daily by oral gavage
- **Treatment window**: Postnatal day 3 (P3) to P21
- **Key results**:
  - **Significant survival improvement** in SMA mice
  - Significant increase in muscle fiber size
  - Significant increase in postsynaptic endplate size (NMJ maturation)
  - Restored normal expression of skeletal muscle development markers
- **Critical mechanistic finding**: Improvement was **NOT mediated by SMN protein upregulation** and did **NOT prevent motor neuron degeneration**
- **Conclusion**: Benefits are primarily **muscle-specific**, underscoring muscle as a therapeutic target in SMA
- **Implication**: ROCK inhibition may complement SMN-restoring therapies (Nusinersen, Risdiplam, Zolgensma) by addressing peripheral/muscle pathology that persists even with SMN correction

#### Bowerman et al. 2014 Review (PMID 25221469)
**"ROCK inhibition as a therapy for spinal muscular atrophy: understanding the repercussions on multiple cellular targets"**
- **Journal**: Frontiers in Neuroscience
- Comprehensive review of RhoA/ROCK pathway roles in SMA-affected cell types:
  - **Neurons**: ROCK hyperactivation impairs axonal growth and regeneration
  - **Myoblasts/Muscle**: Disrupted actin dynamics impair muscle development
  - **Glial cells**: Altered inflammatory signaling
  - **Cardiomyocytes**: Cardiac pathology in SMA
  - **Pancreatic cells**: Metabolic dysfunction in SMA
- ROCK inhibition improved lifespan and skeletal muscle/NMJ pathology **without preventing motor neuron loss**

### Mechanism in SMA Context

The ROCK-LIMK-Cofilin axis is central to SMA pathology:

1. **ROCK hyperactivation** in SMA (due to low SMN) leads to:
   - Increased LIMK2 phosphorylation (LIMK2, not LIMK1, is the relevant kinase in SMA -- see GSE208629 single-cell data)
   - Increased p-Cofilin (inactive cofilin)
   - Excessive F-actin stabilization
   - Impaired actin dynamics

2. **Fasudil inhibits ROCK**, which:
   - Reduces LIMK phosphorylation
   - Restores cofilin activity (dephosphorylation)
   - Normalizes actin turnover
   - Improves cytoskeletal fluidity
   - Enhances NMJ maturation and muscle fiber development

3. **Connection to our single-cell findings** (GSE208629):
   - CFL2 (Cofilin-2) is **upregulated** in SMA motor neurons (+1.83x) -- possibly compensatory
   - LIMK2 (not LIMK1) is the kinase dysregulated in SMA
   - 10/14 actin pathway genes are upregulated in SMA motor neurons
   - ROCK-LIMK2-CFL2 axis = therapeutic target

#### Compassionate Use (PMID 32231638)
- 3 ALS patients treated with IV fasudil 30mg twice daily for 20 consecutive working days
- **Well tolerated, no adverse effects**
- Published: Koch et al., Frontiers in Neurology, 2020

### Safety Profile

**Established safety** from decades of use in Japan/China for cerebral vasospasm:
- **Most common AEs**: Headache, dizziness, facial flushing (all from vasodilation)
- **Key concern: Hypotension** -- fasudil causes vasodilation via ROCK inhibition of vascular smooth muscle
  - Mild/moderate in most patients
  - Requires blood pressure monitoring during IV infusion
  - Oral formulation may have more favorable PK profile
- **AT13148 cautionary tale**: Multi-AGC kinase inhibitor discontinued due to severe hypotension from ROCK inhibition -- demonstrates dose-limiting toxicity of systemic ROCK inhibition
- **ROCK-ALS reassurance**: No clinically significant BP or HR changes in 120-patient Phase 2 trial at 30mg and 60mg doses
- **No serious adverse events attributed to fasudil** in any neurological trial to date

### Combination Potential

#### Monotherapy: Fasudil (ROCK inhibitor)
- **Validated** in SMA preclinical model (Bowerman 2012)
- Addresses muscle pathology and NMJ maturation
- Does NOT restore SMN or prevent MN loss directly
- **Limitation**: Broad ROCK inhibition affects many pathways beyond actin

#### Dual Therapy: Risdiplam + Fasudil
- **Risdiplam**: Restores SMN protein (approved, oral, BBB-penetrant)
- **Fasudil**: Addresses downstream actin/muscle pathology
- **Rationale**: Even with SMN correction, actin pathway dysregulation may persist; fasudil addresses this complementary axis
- **Feasibility**: Both oral, both BBB-penetrant, both with established safety profiles

#### Triple Therapy: Risdiplam + Fasudil + LIMK2 inhibitor
- **Maximal pathway coverage**: SMN restoration + ROCK inhibition + direct LIMK2 inhibition
- **Gap**: No LIMK2-selective inhibitor exists for clinical use (see below)
- **Theoretical advantage**: LIMK2 inhibition would directly prevent cofilin phosphorylation without the vasodilatory side effects of upstream ROCK inhibition

### Critical Gap: No LIMK2-Selective Inhibitor Exists

This is a significant unmet need for SMA therapeutics:

1. **Our single-cell data** (GSE208629) shows LIMK2, not LIMK1, is the relevant kinase dysregulated in SMA motor neurons
2. **LIMK1 and LIMK2 have distinct biological roles**: LIMK1 is predominantly neuronal (growth cone guidance); LIMK2 is more broadly expressed and linked to cell cycle regulation
3. **Dual LIMK1/2 inhibition** (e.g., BMS-5) may have undesirable effects on LIMK1-dependent neuronal processes
4. **T56-LIMKi's claimed LIMK2 selectivity is unreliable** per the 2022 J Med Chem comparative study
5. **Computational approaches** (pharmacoinformatics, structure-based design) are being explored for novel LIMK2-selective inhibitors

**Implication**: Development of a validated LIMK2-selective inhibitor represents a high-value therapeutic opportunity for SMA, particularly in combination with Risdiplam.

## Therapeutic Strategy: ROCK-LIMK2-CFL2 Axis in SMA

### Pathway Logic

```
Low SMN --> RhoA/ROCK hyperactivation --> LIMK2 phosphorylation --> Cofilin-2 phosphorylation (inactive)
                                                                          |
                                                                          v
                                                              Excessive F-actin stabilization
                                                              Impaired actin dynamics
                                                              NMJ immaturity, muscle atrophy
```

### Intervention Points

| Level | Target | Available Drug | Status | Advantage | Limitation |
|-------|--------|---------------|--------|-----------|------------|
| Upstream | SMN restoration | Risdiplam (oral) | **Approved** | Root cause | Does not fully normalize actin pathway |
| Mid-stream | ROCK1/2 | Fasudil (oral) | Phase 2 (ALS, PD) | Validated in SMA preclinical model | Vasodilation side effects; broad target |
| Mid-stream | ROCK2-selective | Belumosudil (oral) | Approved (cGVHD) | ROCK2-selective, oral, approved safety data | BBB penetration unconfirmed for CNS |
| Downstream | LIMK2 | None available | Gap | Direct cofilin regulation, fewer off-targets | No LIMK2-selective compound exists |
| Downstream | LIMK1/2 (dual) | BMS-5 / LIMKi 3 | Research tool | Potent | Not selective; may impair LIMK1 neuronal functions |

### Recommended Research Priorities

1. **Near-term**: Test Fasudil + Risdiplam combination in SMA mouse model (Smn2B/- or severe delta7)
2. **Near-term**: Evaluate Belumosudil (ROCK2-selective, already approved) BBB penetration -- if confirmed, this is a superior candidate to Fasudil for CNS ROCK inhibition
3. **Medium-term**: Develop LIMK2-selective inhibitor via structure-based drug design (crystal structures available)
4. **Long-term**: Triple combination trial (Risdiplam + ROCK inhibitor + LIMK2i) in SMA patients
