# GSE113924 DEG Analysis: Actin Pathway Genes in PFN1-ALS Mouse Model

**Date**: 2026-03-22
**Dataset**: GSE113924 — RNA-Seq of hPFN1-G118V transgenic mouse spinal cord
**Paper**: Fil et al., 2018 (PMID 30213953) — University of Arkansas for Medical Sciences
**Status**: Novel cross-disease analysis. NOT peer-reviewed. Statistical approach is simplified (Welch t-test + BH correction on processed data) vs. proper DESeq2/edgeR on raw counts. Results should be treated as hypothesis-generating.

---

## Summary

We downloaded the processed RNA-seq data from GSE113924 (18,167 genes, 16 samples) and performed differential expression analysis on the **end-stage PFN1-G118V mutant vs. age-matched wildtype** comparison. We then systematically searched for SMA-relevant actin pathway genes that were **never examined in the original publication**.

## The CORO1C Finding (Novel)

**This is the headline result.** CORO1C (Coronin-1C) is significantly upregulated in end-stage PFN1-ALS mouse spinal cord:

- **log2FC = +0.302** (padj = 3.09e-03, MO vs WO)
- **Also significant in disease progression**: log2FC = +0.169 (padj = 4.94e-02, MO vs MY)
- **Concordant with SMA**: CORO1C is elevated in SMA organoids (our platform finding)

**Why this matters:**
1. CORO1C upregulation in SMA was our platform's novel finding (the "double-hit" model: CORO1C as passenger gene on chromosome 12q, potentially affected by copy number or regulatory changes)
2. Nobody has previously reported CORO1C dysregulation in any ALS model
3. The fact that CORO1C goes UP in both SMA and PFN1-ALS suggests a **shared actin stress response** rather than disease-specific regulation
4. CORO1C regulates endosomal actin dynamics and ARP2/3 complex -- both critical for axonal trafficking

**Cross-validation needed**: Check CORO1C in SOD1-G93A (GSE18597), TDP-43 (GSE99354), and C9orf72 models.

## The LIMK1 Finding (Validates ROCK-Cofilin Axis)

LIMK1 is significantly **downregulated** in end-stage PFN1-ALS:

- **log2FC = -0.351** (padj = 5.02e-04, MO vs WO) -- strongest padj of all our targets
- **Also in progression**: log2FC = -0.244 (padj = 9.00e-03, MO vs MY)
- LIMK2 also down: log2FC = -0.171 (padj = 3.02e-03)

**Interpretation**: LIMK1/2 phosphorylate cofilin to inactivate it. LIMK downregulation = more active cofilin = excessive actin depolymerization. This is consistent with the ROCK-LIMK-cofilin axis being disrupted in ALS (sALS patients show paradoxical LIMK1 changes). In SMA, ROCK is hyperactivated, which should increase LIMK activity -- so the direction may differ between diseases, but the pathway is the same.

**Therapeutic angle**: ROCK inhibitors (Fasudil) work by reducing LIMK activity. If LIMK is already down in ALS, ROCK inhibition might need careful dosing to avoid excessive cofilin activation.

---

## The Tmsb4x Finding (Thymosin Beta-4)

**log2FC = +0.777** (padj = 1.71e-02) -- the largest fold change among actin regulators.

Thymosin beta-4 is the major G-actin sequestering protein. Its strong upregulation suggests massive actin monomer buffering, consistent with cytoskeletal collapse at end-stage disease. This has therapeutic relevance: Thymosin beta-4 (Timbetasin/RegeneRx) has been tested in wound healing and cardiac repair.

---

## The PLS3 Finding (Opposite Direction)

PLS3 is **downregulated** in ALS (log2FC = -0.178, padj = 1.11e-02).

This is significant because PLS3 (Plastin-3) is a **protective modifier in SMA** -- patients with higher PLS3 expression have milder disease. PLS3 stabilizes F-actin at the NMJ. Its downregulation in ALS means the actin-stabilizing arm is weakened, contributing to NMJ loss.

**Cross-disease implication**: PLS3 augmentation (already explored for SMA) could also benefit ALS.

Beyond our target genes, 33 actin-related genes were significantly differentially expressed (padj < 0.05):

### Arp2/3 Complex (All Upregulated)
| Gene | log2FC | padj | Role |
|------|--------|------|------|
| Arpc1b | +1.613 | 2.90e-04 | Arp2/3 subunit (strongest signal!) |
| Arpc5 | +0.354 | 4.03e-03 | Arp2/3 subunit |
| Arpc2 | +0.329 | 4.42e-03 | Arp2/3 subunit |
| Arpc4 | +0.203 | 1.10e-02 | Arp2/3 subunit |
| Arpc1a | +0.199 | 1.45e-02 | Arp2/3 subunit |

**Interpretation**: The entire Arp2/3 complex is upregulated. This is the actin nucleation machine. Combined with CORO1C upregulation (which regulates Arp2/3), this suggests a massive attempt to rebuild the actin network at end-stage. CORO1C inhibits Arp2/3-mediated branched actin nucleation, so its co-upregulation may represent a brake mechanism.

**CFL2 note**: The lack of CFL2 dysregulation in PFN1-ALS is actually informative. CFL2 is the muscle-specific cofilin isoform. Its dysregulation in SMA may be SMA-specific (related to SMN's unique role in NMJ maintenance) rather than a general motor neuron disease feature. CFL1 (the neuronal isoform) IS upregulated in PFN1-ALS, consistent with the literature on cofilin activation in ALS.

## Methodological Caveats

1. **Data type**: The GEO supplementary file contains processed/normalized expression values (likely FPKM or TPM, log2-transformed). We used Welch t-test + BH correction. The original paper used DESeq2 on raw counts, which is statistically more rigorous.

2. **Sample size**: 5 MO vs 5 WO (adequate for mouse RNA-seq) and 3 MY vs 3 WY (underpowered).

3. **Our DEG count**: 1,040 DEGs at padj<0.05/|log2FC|>0.5 is consistent with the paper's 836 DEGs, giving confidence our re-analysis is reasonable.

4. **Multiple comparisons**: We tested 26 specific genes. With a Bonferroni threshold of 0.05/26 = 0.0019, LIMK1 (5.02e-04) survives. CORO1C (3.09e-03) is borderline. However, these were hypothesis-driven tests, not genome-wide screening.

5. **Spinal cord tissue**: This is bulk spinal cord RNA-seq, not motor neuron-specific. Gene expression changes could reflect glial infiltration (neuroinflammation) at end-stage rather than motor neuron intrinsic changes.
