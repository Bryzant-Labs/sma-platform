# ROCK/LIMK Inhibitor Landscape -- Summary for News Post

**Date**: 2026-03-24

We compiled a comprehensive landscape of 13 compounds targeting the ROCK-LIMK-Cofilin pathway, the actin regulatory axis increasingly recognized as central to SMA pathology. Among these, **Fasudil** stands out as the lead candidate: it is the only ROCK inhibitor tested in an SMA model (Bowerman 2012, PMID 22397316), where it significantly improved survival and muscle/NMJ maturation in Smn2B/- mice -- notably without restoring SMN protein, suggesting it addresses complementary downstream pathology.

Fasudil's clinical trajectory is accelerating. The **ROCK-ALS Phase 2 trial** (n=120, Lancet Neurology 2024) demonstrated safety in motor neuron disease patients and showed signals of motor unit preservation (MUNIX) and reduced astrogliosis (GFAP). A **Phase 2a trial in Parkinson's** (ROCK-PD) is recruiting, and an **oral formulation** (RT1968) has completed Phase 1 bioavailability testing (~69% oral absorption).

Our single-cell analysis (GSE208629) reveals that **LIMK2 -- not LIMK1 -- is the relevant kinase** in SMA motor neurons, with CFL2 (Cofilin-2) upregulated +1.83x as a likely compensatory response. A critical gap exists: **no LIMK2-selective inhibitor** is available for clinical or even reliable preclinical use (the claimed T56-LIMKi was found inactive in independent validation). This represents a high-value drug development opportunity.

**Therapeutic strategy**: Risdiplam (SMN restoration) + Fasudil (ROCK inhibition) as a near-term testable combination, with LIMK2-selective inhibitor development as a medium-term priority.
